# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People ordering drinks through a DrinkX voice kiosk in a cafe, showroom, investor demo, or laptop prototype. They are standing near a screen, speaking naturally, and need quick confirmation that the AI understood the drink, milk, syrup, sugar, size, and final confirmation.

## Product Purpose

DrinkX AI Barista lets a customer build and confirm a custom drink through voice while the screen mirrors each understood action. Success means the user trusts the assistant, sees the order state update instantly, and can finish the order without navigating a traditional menu.

## Brand Personality

Technical, precise, atmospheric. The interface should feel like a cognitive uplink terminal for coffee ordering: intelligent and premium, but still readable and task-first.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic chatbot layouts, cozy cafe landing pages, AI-purple gradient heroes, decorative card stacks, long instructional copy, and interfaces where the user cannot tell what the assistant has already understood.

## Design Principles

- Voice leads, screen confirms.
- Every visible element must support ordering or system status.
- The interface should feel like a focused terminal, not a marketing page.
- Use strong hierarchy so the current order is always scannable.
- Keep motion tied to listening, thinking, speaking, and confirmation states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target clear contrast, large touch areas, keyboard focus states, and reduced-motion support. Text remains present as backup for noisy environments and for users who cannot rely on audio alone.
